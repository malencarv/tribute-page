Lina Bo Bardi

Itailian-born brazilian modernist architect

Achillina Bo was born in Rome, but moved to Brazil after the second world war events. Lina got inspired by the brazilian vernacular design. She is recognizable for the unique style of the many architectural illustrations she created over her lifetime, along with her tendency to leave poignant notes to herself.

Here is a timeline of Lina Bo Bardi's life

  • 1914: Achillina bo Bardi is born in Rome, Italy;
  • 1939: She graduated from the Rome College of Architecture with her final piece, "The Maternity and Infancy Care Centre";
  • 1939-1943: Lina moved to Milan to begin working with architect Carlo Pagani in the Studio Bo e Pagani;
  • 1942: She opened her own architectural studio on Via Gesù;
  • 1944: Her office was destroyed by an aerial bombing;
  • 1945: She had a deeper involvement in the Italian Communist Party;
  • 1946: Lina bo Bardi travel around South America with her husband Pietro Maria Bardi and go to Brazil;
  • 1947: Assis Chateaubriand invited Pietro Maria Bardi to establish and run a Museum of Art. São Paulo was chosen as the location;
  • 1951: She became a naturalized Brazilian citizen, she also completed her first work, the "Glass House";
  • 1955: Bo Bardi became a lecturer for the Architecture and Urbanism Faculty at the University of São Paulo;
  • 1977: She designed the SESC Pompéia Factory, which was called a "leisure center";
  • 1989: She was honored with the first exhibition of her work at the University of São Paulo;
  • 1992: Lina bo Bardi dies at the Casa de Vidro. She left designs for a new São Paulo City Hall and a Cultural Centre for Vera Cruz.
